    Homemade Meatballs For Your Dog

    Learn how to make meatballs you dog will surely be all over! Learn more

    Leslie G. Ricci - - Staff Contributor

    Obtained her undergraduate in Veterinary Medicine at Colorado State University. Being a life-long dog lover, she's passionate about educating dog owners on making the best purchasing decisions.

    Leslie G. Ricci

    Obtained her undergraduate in Veterinary Medicine at Colorado State University

    When I was little, my grandma used to make the most sumptuous meatballs ever whenever I come to visit her. I really loved it. My pooch Lesley, a beagle, felt the same way as she was always all over my grandma’s meatballs. I will never forget the day I learned the recipe and taught myself how to make one for me and Lesley. One day I thought, why don’t I share the joy and recipe with my friends!

    It was a hit and as expected, their dogs also loved it! I’ve never been so ecstatic to find out a recipe the dogs will surely love that’s a lot healthier and more protein-rich than the average wet dog food and way cheaper to make. Also, what's exciting is you can come up with your own alternatives for the ingredients. Now it's your turn! here’s how to make my grandma’s extraordinary meatballs:

    Grandma’s Meatballs

    • COOKING PROCESS: Oven baked
    • NUTRIENTS: Rich in Protein and fiber
    • DIFFICULTY: Easy
    • PREPARATION TIME: 20 to 30 minutes
    • MATERIALS: You will need to prepare a big bowl for all the ingredients to fit, or you can prepare in smaller batches

    INGREDIENTS:

    5 lbs ground beef or turkey (lean meat)

    2 slices of bread cubed small

    1 cup of oat bran (preferably low carb)

    2 eggs

    1 1/2 cans of pumpkin or sweet potato puree

    2 carrots boiled / steamed and mashed

    Leaves of 2 kale stalks chopped finely (you may

    use collard greens or english peas with the same

    amount)

    Leaves of 2 kale stalks chopped finely (you may use collard greens or english peas with the same amount)

    Flour

    Pinch of salt

    Olive oil (optional)

    DIRECTIONS:

    Step 1: Preheat oven to 400F

    Step 2: Put all ingredients in a large bowl. Mix and knead them altogether until thoroughly combined and form them into any size of balls you'd like. I recommend preparing it in the size your dog can gobble up and chew properly as some dogs may have a hard time to chew big chunks.

    Step 3: Dredge the balls lightly with the flour and shake off any excess. You can spray the batch with olive oil for more caramelized texture.

    Step 4: Put the meatballs on a baking sheet in 400F oven until they are done. Bake time will depend on the size of the meatballs; usually mine only take about 20-25 min. (mine are about the size of a munchkins).

    To know that the meatballs are already cooked, they should be evenly cooked and nicely brown on the outside.

    Step 5: You’re done! Meatballs are best served when they’re still warm. Enjoy!

    STORAGE:

    Store the meatballs in a zip lock resealable bag and put them in a freezer for longer shelf life. Remember that a properly stored meatballs in the freezer will last for 3 months.
