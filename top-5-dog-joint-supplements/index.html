According to the College of Life Sciences, UCD Dublin, dogs who received a combination of glucosamine and chondroitin had statistically significant improvements showing reductions in pain and the severity of the condition.5

The best glucosamine for dogs is made from the shells of crustaceans such as shrimp, crab, and crayfish, while chondroitin is sourced from animal cartilage. When in supplement form, check if the manufacturer follows the precise ratio of these ingredients to each other. This will help avoid certain brands with imbalanced formulation that can cause adverse effects to your dog’s health.

Thus, we must keep an eye out for these ingredients when comparing different supplements. If a product lacks these essentials, it’s a deal breaker. As always, please consult with your veterinarian before feeding your dog any supplement to better understand his or her specific needs. INGREDIENTS TO AVOID IN DOG JOINT SUPPLEMENTS

  • Soy - It is one of the top food allergens in dogs. It should be avoided especially when dog has food allergies as it links to a few health problems such as very low thyroid hormone levels.14
  • Corn Syrup - Like sugar, feeding your dog with large amounts of it can result in obesity, short lifespan, multiple secondary diagnoses including diabetes.15
  • Ethoxyquin - It is a synthetic food preservative that is considered to be responible for reproductive problems, autoimmune disorders, and various types of cancers in dogs.16
  • Propylene Glycol - In large doses, it can cause nervous system issues and lactic acidosis. Common symptoms include seizures, tremors, panting, anemia, and lethargy.17

KEY INGREDIENTS TO LOOK FOR IN DOG JOINT SUPPLEMENTS

KEY INGREDIENTS TO LOOK FOR IN DOG JOINT SUPPLEMENTS

  • Glucosamine: This compound occurs naturally and is made up of Glucose (a sugar) and Glutamine (an amino acid). It helps with creating molecules that are vital in the formation of joint cartilage so as to boost mobility and flexibility.3
  • Chondroitin: This is the main sulfate component of cartilage. It enhances cartilage elasticity, helps to retain sufficient properties for shock absorption and promotes retention of water in the joint.6
  • MSM: Otherwise known as Methylsulfonylmethane, this is a form of sulphur and is highly beneficial for easing joint pain, canine arthritis, and joint inflammation.7
  • Turmeric: It contains curcumin, which inhibits enzymes that are active in the process of inflammation that eventually results in painful joints. It also has antioxidant properties and antioxidants reduce damage to joint cell walls, thus reducing pain. 8
  • Green Lipped Mussel: It is a great dietary supplement for muscles, nervous system and the heart, and also ensures healthy bones, thanks to a significant amount of calcium. Similarly, it is used in treating hypocalcemia, a condition of low calcium levels.9
  • Hyaluronic Acid: It is a main component of both the cartilage and synovial fluid that bathes the joints. The synovial fluid binds to the water and creates a thick gelatinous substance that lubricates the cartilage and protects it from wear.6
  • Calcium Carbonate: This is one of the best sources of calcium and an essential supplement for healthy joints, muscles and strong bones. Your dog’s body cannot produce calcium on its own and derives it from food.10
