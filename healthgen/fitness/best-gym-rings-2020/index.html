What Gym Rings Do and Why Are They Important?

Gymnastic rings are one of the most versatile pieces of equipment available to fitness enthusiasts. And when they’re put to use, they offer a great deal of benefits. Training with gymnastics rings is no doubt one of the most effective ways of stabilizing muscles and building strength as well. Given that they’re very unstable, the body whas to compensate for this by striving to maintain balance, and this helps you to build your stability. 

Gym rings are important to athletes and fitness enthusiasts because of specific benefits like proprioception, boosting performance transference and of course, upper body strength muscle gains. The instability of the gym ring forces you to tap into your core and balance, building endurance in your arms, and stability and strength in your body. The muscles that are mostly impacted include the lats, rhomboids, deltoids, biceps, abdominals, forearms, and even the pecs get worked.

What To Avoid in Choosing Gym Rings

In choosing gym rings, certain factors should be looked out for in order to avoid sour incidents. Usually, these products have benchmarks that they’re expected to meet up to in terms of the various features that sport. Here are some of the most important things to avoid when selecting a gym ring:

  • Weak Straps - Poor quality straps can suddenly cut and cause you injury. This is why you need straps that are made with thick nylon webbing. 
  • Poor Quality Materials - Look out for rings that are made with sturdy wood, high quality plastic or steel. 
  • Lack of Impressive Features - An impressive ring should come with straps within the range of 15’ to 18'. In addition, durability cannot be discounted for.  

What To Look Out for Gym Rings

What you should look out for in gym rings can be found in the section that outlines the features of any products, down to its limits and capabilities. For instance, an essential feature is size and the standard thickness is set at between 1 and 1.25 inches. The grip is also essential among other factors that are itemized below. 

  • Features- You need to ensure that the gym rings you are going with offer the best features. Look out for the grip and the materials that the rings are made with. 
  • Safety/ Durability- It’s no use buying equipment that would cause you injury. Safety is important. A good measure to take is to go for rings made out of steel or wood or high quality plastic. 
  • Grip- The importance of grip that gym rings offer cannot be overstated. You should ensure that the grip is firm. For instance, if you would be going with a plastic ring, go for polycarbonate textured rings. For wooden rings, the grip is usually top-notch. 
  • Size- Generally speaking, rings should have a thickness anywhere between 1 and 1.25 inches. Another factor is the size of your hands. 
  • Straps & Buckles- These should be long enough for you to hang from reasonable height ranges. Look for straps that range from 15’ to 18'. 

Plastic vs Wood

Plastic and wood gym rings serve the same basic function. However, there are certain features that set each apart. First off, if you’re going to be doing a lot of your workouts outside, plastic gym rings hold up better and can brave the elements. Wooden gym rings on the other hand can get damaged easily especially if there is continuous exposure to sun and moisture. 

Now, for the actual workout. Muscle ups on wooden rings lead to less pronounced skin damage than their plastic counterpart. Also, wooden rings are easier to grip. Once you start sweating while using a plastic ring, they would remain moist almost throughout the session and you would have to continually wipe down. 

So, in terms of grip and the effects on your hands, wooden rings are more preferable.

Strap vs Buckle

Some people seem to prefer that their gym rings come with buckles so that they can be adjusted very easily to various heights depending on the workout that they have in mind. However, there is a downside to this and it lies in the buckle itself. Poor quality buckles can come off at any time and even high quality ones begin to wear after a while. 

Using just straps on the other hand takes away the nagging fear of the buckle coming loose and of having to release and tighten buckles. To work with straps, they would have to be of the best possible quality. A great option is a 1.5’ heavy duty military grade webbing strap. Now, this is one that can hold its own anywhere and anytime.
